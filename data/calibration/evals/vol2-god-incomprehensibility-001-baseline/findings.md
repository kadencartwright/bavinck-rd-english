# Reviewer Findings

## Summary

The translation maintains glossary fidelity and correctly preserves Greek, Hebrew, and Latin spans while accurately converting Dutch Scripture references into standard English forms. However, the English prose contains a significant grammatical error in paragraph 1 that inverts the theological meaning regarding God's namelessness, alongside several syntactic calques that compromise formal theological register.

## Findings

- [high] doctrinal-ambiguity: Paragraph 1 translates 'Hĳ is niet te beschrĳven en met geen naam te noemen' as 'He cannot be described and named by no name.' The double negative 'cannot... no name' logically implies He *can* be named, contradicting the source which affirms He cannot be named by any name.
- [medium] prose-quality: Paragraph 1 translates 'In zooverre is het alles mysterie' as 'Insofar it is all mystery.' 'Insofar' requires a following clause (e.g., 'insofar as') or should be rendered 'In this respect' to match the Dutch usage and standard English theological prose.
- [low] prose-quality: Paragraph 2 uses the construction 'Also Greek philosophy has...' which mirrors the Dutch 'Ook de Grieksche...' syntax; standard English prefers 'Greek philosophy also has...' or 'Greek philosophy, too, has...'.

## Recommended Follow-Up

- Correct the double negative in the final sentence of paragraph 1 to 'He cannot be described or named by any name.'
- Revise 'Insofar it is all mystery' to 'In this respect, everything is mystery.'
- Adjust Dutchicisms such as 'Also Greek philosophy has...' for smoother English flow.
