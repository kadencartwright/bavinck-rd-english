# Reviewer Findings

## Summary

The translation preserves Greek, Hebrew, Latin, and German spans correctly and renders Dutch Scripture references in standard English forms. Glossary terms are properly applied throughout. However, the Dutch word 'adaequaat' is left untranslated in section 3, and German words 'Thatsachen' and 'Rhetorik' embedded in Dutch prose should be translated to 'facts' and 'rhetoric' for English readers. An awkward English construction also requires revision.

## Findings

- [high] untranslated-term: Dutch word 'adaequaat' in section 3 is left untranslated as 'adaequaat'; should be rendered 'adequate' in English.
- [medium] untranslated-term: German words 'Thatsachen' and 'Rhetorik' in section 1 are embedded in Dutch prose (not German quotations) and should be translated to 'facts' and 'rhetoric' for English readers.
- [low] prose-quality: Awkward construction 'He cannot be described and called by no name' should be revised to 'He cannot be described, nor can He be called by any name' or similar for grammatical clarity.

## Recommended Follow-Up

- Translate 'adaequaat' to 'adequate' in section 3.
- Translate 'Thatsachen' to 'facts' and 'Rhetorik' to 'rhetoric' in section 1.
- Revise awkward construction for better English prose flow.
