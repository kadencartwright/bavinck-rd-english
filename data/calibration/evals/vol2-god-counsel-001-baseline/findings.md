# Reviewer Findings

## Summary

The translation successfully preserves Greek, Hebrew, Latin, and German spans and adheres to the glossary term 'counsel of God', while correctly standardizing most Scripture references into English forms. However, the prose quality check fails due to the retention of Dutch abbreviations such as 'd. i.' and 'blz.' which the rubric explicitly treats as defects, and the review flagging identifies these lapses in standardizing non-English abbreviations.

## Findings

- [high] Untranslated Dutch: The Dutch abbreviation 'd. i.' (dat is) is retained in the translation ('opera essentialia ad intra, d. i. the decrees') instead of being rendered as 'i.e.'; note that the translator correctly used 'i.e.' in later instances, indicating inconsistency.
- [high] Untranslated Dutch: The Dutch abbreviation 'blz.' (bladzijde) for page numbers is retained in the citation 'Kuenen, Volksgodsd. en wereldgodsd. 1882 blz. 40 v.' instead of being translated to 'p.' or 'pp.'.
- [medium] Untranslated Dutch: The Dutch abbreviation 'v.' (volgende) for 'following' is retained in the citation 'blz. 40 v.' instead of the standard English 'f.' or 'ff.'.

## Recommended Follow-Up

- Replace the retained 'd. i.' with 'i.e.' to ensure consistency with the rest of the translation.
- Translate 'blz.' to 'p.' and 'v.' to 'f.' or 'ff.' in all bibliographic citations.
- Verify that all Dutch bibliographic titles are handled according to project preferences (translated or kept original with translation in brackets).
