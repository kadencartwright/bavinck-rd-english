# Reviewer Findings

## Summary

The translation adheres to the glossary and preserves most foreign language spans, but fails to preserve the Latin phrase 'a reali potentiae immensitate' and inconsistently translates Latin names (Cartesius, Hieronymus) while preserving others. The English prose contains grammatical errors ('avoided of God', 'on different manner') and a significant doctrinal ambiguity regarding God's origin ('He is from nothing').

## Findings

- [high] preserved-language-integrity: The Latin span 'a reali potentiae immensitate' was translated as 'from a real immensity of potency' instead of being preserved exactly as required by the style guide.
- [medium] prose-quality: Grammatical error in 'the expression causa sui was avoided of God'; the preposition 'of' incorrectly implies agency, whereas the source 'van God' indicates the subject matter (correct: 'avoided regarding God').
- [medium] doctrinal-ambiguity: The phrase 'Hij is van niets' was translated as 'He is from nothing', which risks implying creation ex nihilo applies to God; the context of aseitas requires a rendering like 'He is dependent on nothing'.

## Recommended Follow-Up

- Restore the Latin phrase 'a reali potentiae immensitate' and review all Latin names (e.g., Cartesius, Hieronymus) for consistent preservation.
- Correct the grammatical construction 'avoided of God' and the phrase 'on different manner' to standard English formal prose.
- Revise 'He is from nothing' to eliminate the ambiguity regarding God's origin and ensure it reflects His independence.
