# Style Guide

- Preserve all Greek, Hebrew, Latin, and German spans exactly as they appear in the source excerpt.
- Retain citation structure, but translate Dutch Scripture references, book names, and abbreviations into standard English forms; keep patristic or scholastic citations intact.
- Translate into formal but readable English theological prose; do not modernize into colloquial language.
- Prefer glossary-approved renderings when the glossary lists a source phrase from the excerpt.
- Flag ambiguous doctrinal phrasing instead of smoothing over uncertainty.
